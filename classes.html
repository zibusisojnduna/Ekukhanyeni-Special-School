Our Curricilum

Our curriculum has three parts to it. Those are:

  • DCAPS, which focuses mainly on Grade 1 to Grade 3 students.
  • LSPID, which focuses on students from Grade 1 to Grade 4.
  • And Skills Development, which focuses on Grade 4 to Grade 5.

What is DCAPS?

DCAPS is an abbreviation for Differentiated Curriculum And Assessment Policy Statement. At its core it is the CAPS system used across South Africa but in this case it is slightly adjusted for our students with disabilies. This programme is structured in such a way that it makes provisions for a large scale of learners with moderate to severe intellectual disabilty and learning difficulties across Grade 1 to 3. The other goal is to help develop their perosnality, talents and creativity, as well as ther mental and physical abilities.


What is LSPID?

LSPID is the Learning Programme (LP) for children with Severe to Profound Intellectual Disability (SPID). The goal of this programme is to asses the learning needs (strengths and weeknesses) of children with SPID, plan intergrated programmes and activities to ensure maximum development and community intergration and intergrate the Learning Programme into the Daily Programme by means of illustrative examples.


Skills Development

From Grade 4 to Grade 5 our students mostly focus on improving skills and developing new ones.These include the likes of leatherwork, woodwork and printing. The students also take part in the agricultrural side of skills including gardening, sewing and garmet making. This is done in the hopes that they can be prepared for life outside of the classroom.
